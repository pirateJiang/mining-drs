Net Present Value (NPV): The total current value of all future cash flows (revenues minus costs) generated by a project over its lifetime, discounted back to the present day to account for the time value of money. In mining, it is the ultimate metric for project profitability. In your code (metrics.py), calc_npv does exactly this by discounting profit at each time step.  

Mineral Value Chain: The complete sequence of integrated operations involved in producing a saleable metal from the ground. It includes mining, crushing, grinding (comminution), separation (flotation), and metallurgy.  

Blending: The practice of mixing different ore types (e.g., Ore 1 and Ore 2) from various stockpiles in specific weight fractions to create a consistent, predictable feed stream for the concentrator.  

Simulation-Based Optimization: Using a dynamic simulator (like Rockwell Arena or your custom Python DES) in a loop with an optimization engine (like OptQuest or RL) to search for optimal control parameters.  

Strategic Algorithm: An algorithm that operates on a high level of abstraction over long time horizons (years/decades) to determine the optimal sequence to extract blocks from the ground and maximize NPV.
